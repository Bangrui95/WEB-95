UAL CCI Summer Show

Synesthetic Garden is an interactive installation that combines holographic projection, real-time environmental sensing, and generative visual systems. The project explores the relationship between natural environments, human perception, and digital systems by transforming real-world environmental data into dynamic visual expressions.

The installation uses an Arduino-based sensor system to collect real-time environmental data, including light intensity, temperature, humidity, and human activity. This data is transmitted to TouchDesigner, where it is processed and mapped into a generative visual system. Through algorithmic structures and particle-based graphics, environmental signals are translated into continuously evolving visual forms.

By integrating Arduino sensing systems, TouchDesigner, and holographic projection, the project creates an immersive audiovisual environment. It investigates how environmental data can shape digital visual systems and explores new possibilities for translating data-driven processes into spatial and physical artistic experiences.

Team Collaboration

The project was developed through a collaborative team process. Team members were responsible for designing and building the Arduino-based interactive hardware system, while I focused on receiving real-time sensor data from Arduino and processing it in TouchDesigner. Multiple data channels were processed in parallel, allowing inputs from different sensors to be integrated and translated into real-time generative visual outputs.
