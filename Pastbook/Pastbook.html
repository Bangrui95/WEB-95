The Past

This project focuses on a historical architectural district in Dalian known as Liansuo Street. During the period of Japanese colonial rule, the area was once one of the most prosperous commercial districts in East Asia. After the end of colonial governance, the district returned to Chinese administration. However, due to complex historical legacies and shifting urban development priorities, the area gradually declined and now exists in a state of physical deterioration and social neglect.

Through field research, archival investigation, and the collection of physical materials, the project extracts textures embedded in walls, signage, building surfaces, and discarded fragments. These traces quietly document a contested historical period. Rather than treating these textures as decorative elements, the project regards them as carriers of time, political memory, and everyday life.

The collected visual materials are reorganized into a book that reconstructs the narrative of the street through graphic interpretation and archival storytelling. By transforming urban decay into a tactile publication, the project reframes the district as a living historical document. It calls for renewed public awareness of this overlooked chapter of regional history and questions how cities remember, erase, or preserve their colonial past.
